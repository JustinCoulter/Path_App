    • Work your path


  • When you're ready to work on your most important path questions, move these cards to the actions list
  • On the back on these cards, post the actions you will take to answer each question on the actions list
  • As you begin to get answers to each question, move that question card to the answers list
  • As you get answers from your actions, post these answers on the answers list
  • As you move along your path, keep updating your knowns, path questions, actions, and answers lists
  • Update your opportunity question if and when it seems useful to do so


    • A Path Method Glossary


  • Actions: anything you will do (visualize, ask, search, try, and so on) to answer a path question
  • Answers: anything you discover, learn, and accomplish from any actions along a path
  • Benefits: the potential tangible and intangible, long and short term, self and other serving advantages of working on your opportunity question
  • Knowns: anything that is true, fact, confirmed, researched, decided
  • Opportunity question: a "How can I..." question used to organize a path
  • Path: a responsive sequence of questions used to organize any kind of uncertainty
  • Path questions: any kinds of questions that represent actionable unknowns on a path
  • Sequence: the order in which you will work on path questions; the most important (unanswered) path questions are always at the top
